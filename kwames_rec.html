Recipe of Kwame's Escovitch Snapper.
  1. Pickled Vegetables
  2. 2 medium-size yellow onions, thinly sliced
  3. 2 large carrots, cut into thin strips
  4. 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar
  5. 5 large garlic cloves, minced
  6. 4 teaspoons minced peeled fresh ginger
  7. 1 fresh Scotch bonnet chile, stemmed, unseeded, and thinly sliced (about 1 tablespoon)
  8. 2 teaspoons fresh lime juice
  9. 2 teaspoons ground allspice
  10. 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  11. Fish
  12. 1 cup canola oil, plus more for frying
  13. 20 large garlic cloves
  14. 2 tablespoons seasoned salt
  15. 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  16. 1 (2-inch) piece peeled fresh ginger
  17. 2 (3- to 3 1/2-pound) whole red snapper, scaled and gutted
  18. 2 teaspoons kosher salt
Directions

Combine onions, carrots, vinegar, garlic, ginger, chile, lime juice, allspice, and salt in a large bowl; toss to combine. Cover and refrigerate 6 to 24 hours.

Combine oil, garlic, seasoned salt, thyme, and ginger in a blender; process until a smooth paste forms, about 2 minutes. Using a sharp knife, score fish skin in a crosshatch pattern. Rub oil mixture evenly on skin and in cavities of fish. Place fish on a large baking sheet; cover tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate about 2 hours.

Fill a large Dutch oven (wide enough to fit 1 whole snapper horizontally) with oil to a depth of 4 inches. Heat over medium-high until oil reaches 350Â°F. Sprinkle fish evenly with kosher salt. Fry fish, 1 at a time, until browned and cooked through, about 10 minutes, carefully turning halfway through cook time. Remove from pot, and transfer to a serving platter. Top with pickled vegetables.
